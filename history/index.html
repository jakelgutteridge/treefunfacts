History

King James I

Cedars Park is the former site of Theobalds Palace, the favourite residence of King James I.

Theobalds originated as a smaller estate in the 14th century, but was expanded by Sir William Cecil for Queen Elizabeth I, who stayed there around 15 times.

The house was later acquired by James I, who turned it into a grand palace with a 2500-acre deer park. For much of the Jacobean period, England was run right from these grounds, as the country was an absolute monarchy and Theobalds was the King's favourite residence.

Theobalds Palace was largely demolished during the English Civil War, and the grounds were donated to the public in 1919 as a public park. The park was very small at this time and was expanded in the 1960s following the purchase of Old Palace House. In 1999, wildflower meadows were added at the south, and today the park covers 19 acres. (This is still relatively small and as a result, there is a prominent sense of community at Cedars Park.)
