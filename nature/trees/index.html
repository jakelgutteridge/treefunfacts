Trees

Cedars Park contains a mature and diverse collection of trees from around the world. Between 2019 and 2021, volunteers from the Friends of Cedars Park carried out a Tree Identification Project — all of the main trees have now been identified and numbered, effectively creating a formal arboretum with a tree listing that allows people to easily see the species of each tree.

The current temporary tree tags were installed in 2019 and we are due to install permanent tags in 2023.

Tree identification

CPRE Hertfordshire said: 'The tree project is a valuable asset helping visitors to discover the trees in the park. Their species lists and measurements . . . have contributed to our national knowledge of trees in cultivation'.

To see the species of all trees in the arboretum, refer to the Cedars Park Arboretum Tree Listing.

Note: This Tree Listing only covers trees within the designated Arboretum Zones, which cover the majority of the park. If you would like to identify a tree outside of this area, such as in heavy woodland or the Wildflower Meadows, we have produced a basic Tree Identification Guide.

As part of the tree project, we have also collated some Tree Fun Facts to complement this information.
