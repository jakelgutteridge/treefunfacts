Nature

Cedars Park is biodiverse with a strong mix of grassland, wildflower meadows and high-rise woodland that contributes to a very good environment for most native species that reside here. Broxbourne Borough Council considers the wildlife of the park in the annual Management and Maintenance Plan, e.g. one of their objectives is to 'maintain existing and create new habitats where appropriate'.

The Friends of Cedars Park commissioned Herts & Middlesex Wildlife Trust to carry out a Wildlife Survey of the park in 2017 (see below) and they confirmed that the park provides considerable resources for wildlife to forage, shelter, and nest in.
